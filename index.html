🔍 Phase 1 of DEEDS

Where curiosity meets consciousness.

Scholars see, hear, and feel a challenge before they study it, building empathy, connection, and social consciousness. This creates a compelling personal "why" for the learning and action to come.

By the End
Success Criteria

By the end of Discover, students should demonstrate growth in three core skill areas:

1

Frame and Define the Challenge

Ask questions and use evidence to name a real-world problem worth investigating.

NGSS SEP 1, SEP 4 · C3 D4.6 · CCSS R.7
2

Understand Human Impact

Examine different perspectives to understand who is affected and how.

C3 D2.Civ.10 · CCSS R.6
3

Articulate Purpose & Significance

Make evidence-based claims about why the problem is important and what should change.

NGSS SEP 7 · CCSS W.1, W.9, SL.4

Use the Developmental Continuum

The continuum describes how students' ability to understand a challenge and make evidence-based claims about its causes and impacts develops over time. Because it is used across multiple DEEDS challenges, it helps teachers track growth, give targeted feedback, and see how students' reasoning deepens from one project to the next.

Overview
See How It Works

Discover mirrors how people encounter, understand, and begin to care about real-world challenges, both in school and in the wider world.

🌍

Experience the Challenge

Meet the challenge through a provocation or real-world experience.

→
🗣️

Understand Perspectives

Hear from people affected and explore different viewpoints.

→
🔎

Explore Evidence

Investigate data, examples, and patterns related to the challenge.

→
🧩

Make Meaning

Reflect on what you've learned and why it matters to you or your community.

→
📢

Explain Why It Matters

Make an evidence-based claim about the significance of the challenge.

💡 The Teacher Mindset

You're not teaching content — you're curating wonder. Design moments that make scholars lean in and say, "Wait, why is this happening?"

Think documentary filmmaker: make the challenge impossible to ignore.

What Scholars Do

👁️
See

Images, videos, data

👂
Hear

Stories, testimonials

🤲
Feel

Simulations, role-play, walks

💬
Respond

Reflect, question, connect

🤝 Community Partners

During the Discover phase, community partners help students understand the real-world challenge from multiple perspectives. Partners may engage in several ways:

👥
Clients or community members experiencing the challenge firsthandhelping students understand the human impact of the issue.
🏢
Organizations already working on the challengesharing what actions are being taken and where needs still exist.
🔬
Professionals or expertsoffering technical knowledge or industry perspectives that deepen students' understanding.
📍
Field sites or community locationswhere students can visit, observe the challenge in context, and ask questions directly.

Together, these experiences help students ground the challenge in real life and begin identifying the questions they need to investigate next.

"How can we create calming toys that help pets feel calm while they wait to be adopted?"

Students learn that shelter dogs often feel anxious, which can prevent them from getting adopted. Over 5 sessions, they experience the challenge firsthand, examine evidence, and make a case for why solving it matters.
